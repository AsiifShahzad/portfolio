Mall Customer Segmentation

A data-driven approach to identify distinct customer groups based on purchasing behaviors and demographic information, enabling targeted marketing strategies.

Project Overview

This project performs customer segmentation analysis on the Mall Customers dataset from Kaggle. By applying unsupervised machine learning techniques, we identify natural groupings of customers with similar characteristics and behaviors.

Dataset

The Mall Customers dataset from Kaggle contains information about customers visiting a shopping mall, including demographic details and a spending score that reflects their purchasing behavior.

Methodology

1

Data Preprocessing & Exploration

The first step involved cleaning the dataset, handling missing values, and performing exploratory data analysis to understand the distribution of features and identify potential patterns. This included analyzing the demographic profile of customers and their spending behaviors.

2

Feature Analysis & Correlation

We analyzed the relationships between different features using correlation matrices and visualizations. This helped us understand how factors like age, income, and gender relate to spending patterns, providing initial insights into customer behavior.

3

Determining Optimal Clusters

Using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis, we determined the optimal number of clusters for our segmentation. This step is crucial for ensuring that our customer segments are distinct and meaningful.

4

K-Means Clustering

We applied the K-Means clustering algorithm to segment customers based on their annual income and spending score. This unsupervised learning technique grouped customers with similar characteristics together, revealing natural patterns in the data.

5

Visualization & Interpretation

The final step involved visualizing the customer segments and interpreting the results. We created various plots to represent the clusters and analyzed the characteristics of each segment to derive actionable insights for targeted marketing strategies.

Key Insights

High Income, High Spenders

Cluster 1 represents customers with high income and high spending scores. This premium segment is ideal for luxury product marketing and premium services.

High Income, Low Spenders

Cluster 2 includes customers with high income but low spending scores. This segment has high potential and could be targeted with loyalty programs and exclusive offers.

Average Income & Spending

Cluster 3 represents the average customers with moderate income and spending. This is the largest segment and should be the focus of general marketing campaigns.

Low Income, High Spenders

Cluster 4 includes customers with low income but high spending scores. This segment is price-sensitive but values shopping experiences, making them ideal for sales and promotions.

Low Income, Low Spenders

Cluster 5 represents customers with low income and low spending scores. This segment requires budget-friendly options and value-based marketing approaches.

Gender-Based Insights

Female customers have a slightly higher average spending score (51.5) compared to male customers (48.8), suggesting gender-specific marketing strategies could be effective.
